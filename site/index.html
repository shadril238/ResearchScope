⚙️ How ResearchScope Picks Your 1,000 Papers

Every day we scan 50,000+ papers across arXiv CoRR and 15+ top conference proceedings. Here's how we narrow it down to the 1,000 worth your time.

🌐
Step 1 — Collect
Pull every new CS paper from arXiv CoRR (19 categories) + proceedings from NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, ACL and 10+ more venues
🏆
Step 2 — Score
Each paper gets a Paper Score 0–10 based on 6 signals: recency, novelty, citation quality, institution prestige, author reputation, and completeness
✂️
Step 3 — Curate
Take the top 500 arXiv papers and top 500 conference papers by score — ensuring you always get both fresh preprints and peer-reviewed work
🔄
Step 4 — Refresh
arXiv papers update daily (Mon–Fri). Conference papers are permanent — added once per proceedings cycle, never expire

📊 The Scoring System — Plain English

📅 Recency (28%)

A paper published this year scores 10/10. One from 10+ years ago scores 0. CS moves fast — freshness matters.

💡 Novelty (22%)

We scan the title and abstract for words like "we propose", "outperforms", "state-of-the-art". Surveys and replications score lower.

📈 Citation Quality (18%)

Uses log-scaled citation count + conference rank (A* = top-tier). A paper at NeurIPS with 500 citations scores near 10.

🏛️ Institution Prestige (15%)

Papers from OpenAI, DeepMind, Google Research, MIT, Stanford, CMU etc. get a boost. Detected from affiliations or abstract text.

👤 Author Reputation (10%)

Papers co-authored by Turing Award winners, lab founders, or well-known researchers (Hinton, LeCun, Vaswani, Karpathy…) score higher.

✅ Completeness (7%)

Has the paper got an abstract, author list, PDF link, and topic tags? Well-documented papers surface above poorly indexed ones.

🚀 Author Momentum — Who's on a Hot Streak?

Author momentum measures how productive and impactful a researcher has been recently — not just their all-time record. It combines:

  • 📝 Recent output (30%) — papers published in the last 2 years
  • ⭐ Avg paper quality (25%) — mean Paper Score across all their work
  • 📈 Acceleration (20%) — publishing faster than their prior 2-year window?
  • 🔥 Topic strength (15%) — are they working in trending areas (LLMs, diffusion, etc.)?
  • 🏆 Conference strength (10%) — what % of their papers are at A* venues?

Why momentum, not just citations?

A senior professor with 10,000 citations but no papers in 3 years scores low. A PhD student publishing 4 strong LLM papers this year at NeurIPS scores high. Momentum finds who's actively pushing the frontier right now — which is what matters if you want to follow the cutting edge.

💬 Why not just use citation count? Citation counts lag reality by 1–2 years, heavily favour older papers, and ignore emerging researchers entirely. Our multi-signal scoring surfaces important new work before the citation wave hits.
